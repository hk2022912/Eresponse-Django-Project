Jaw Injury Bandage Technique

Step-by-Step Instructions:

  1. Ensure the patient is sitting comfortably and can breathe properly.
  2. Place the center of the bandage under the chin.
  3. Bring both ends up and cross them over the top of the head.
  4. Take the ends around to the back of the head and cross them again.
  5. Bring the ends forward and secure with a knot or safety pin at the front.
  6. Check that the bandage is secure but not too tight.
  7. Monitor the patient for any signs of breathing difficulty.

Protecting Yourself During an Earthquake

What to Do During an Earthquake:

  • Drop: Get down on your hands and knees to prevent falling.
  • Cover: Cover your head and neck with your arms. If possible, crawl under a sturdy desk or table.
  • Hold On: Hold on to your shelter until the shaking stops.
  • If there's no table or desk nearby, crouch against an interior wall away from windows.
  • Stay indoors until the shaking stops and you're sure it's safe to exit.
  • If you're outdoors, move to a clear area away from buildings, trees, and power lines.

First Aid for Fractures

How to Treat a Suspected Fracture:

  1. Assess the situation: Check for bleeding, deformity, and pain level.
  2. Immobilize the area: Prevent movement of the injured body part.
  3. Apply a splint if available: Use firm materials to stabilize the fracture.
  4. Control bleeding: Apply gentle pressure with a clean cloth if necessary.
  5. Apply cold compress: Reduce swelling with an ice pack (15 minutes on, 15 minutes off).
  6. Elevate the injury: If possible, raise the injured area above heart level.
  7. Seek medical help: All suspected fractures should be evaluated by medical professionals.

The Earthquake Preparedness Plan

Before an Earthquake:

  • Create an emergency plan and share it with all household members.
  • Prepare an emergency kit with water, food, medications, and essential supplies.
  • Secure heavy furniture and appliances to walls.
  • Know where and how to shut off utilities (gas, water, electricity).
  • Identify safe spots in each room (under sturdy furniture, against interior walls).
  • Practice earthquake drills regularly with your family or household.

After an Earthquake:

  • Check for injuries and provide first aid if needed.
  • Inspect your home for damage, especially gas leaks and electrical issues.
  • Listen to emergency radio for instructions and updates.
  • Expect and prepare for aftershocks.
  • Use phones only for emergency calls.

Basic CPR: The Chain of Survival

Adult CPR Steps:

  1. Check for responsiveness: Tap the person and shout "Are you okay?"
  2. Call for help: Have someone call emergency services immediately.
  3. Check for breathing: Look, listen, and feel for breathing for no more than 10 seconds.
  4. Begin chest compressions: Place hands in center of chest, compress at least 2 inches deep.
  5. Compression rate: Push hard and fast at a rate of 100-120 compressions per minute.
  6. Rescue breaths: After 30 compressions, give 2 rescue breaths (if trained).
  7. Continue CPR: Maintain cycles of 30 compressions and 2 breaths until help arrives.
  8. Use AED: If available, turn it on and follow the verbal instructions.

Remember:

  • Proper hand position is key: center of chest, between nipples
  • Allow chest to fully recoil between compressions
  • Minimize interruptions in compressions
  • If untrained, perform hands-only CPR (compressions without rescue breaths)

Home Fire Safety and Evacuation Planning

Fire Prevention Tips:

  • Install smoke alarms on every level of your home and test them monthly.
  • Keep flammable items at least three feet from heat sources.
  • Never leave cooking unattended.
  • Check electrical cords for damage and don't overload outlets.
  • Keep portable heaters at least three feet from anything that can burn.
  • Have your heating system serviced annually by a qualified professional.

Creating a Home Escape Plan:

  1. Draw a floor plan of your home showing all possible exits.
  2. Mark two ways out of each room, including windows and doors.
  3. Designate a meeting place outside where everyone will gather.
  4. Practice your fire drill both during day and night.
  5. If smoke is present, get low and go under the smoke to exit.
  6. Remember: Once you get out, stay out. Never go back inside a burning building.
